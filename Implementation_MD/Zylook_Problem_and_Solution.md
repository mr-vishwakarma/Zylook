# 2. Problem & Solution

*Zylook — AI-Powered Outfit Commerce Platform*

## 2.1 Core Problem

Traditional fashion e-commerce forces users to manually assemble an outfit one item at a time. Every additional step — finding a shirt, going back, finding a pant, trying to match it, finding shoes — introduces friction and a fresh chance to abandon the session entirely.

**Traditional Fashion E-Commerce Flow:**

```
User lands → Searches product → Adds shirt → Goes back →
Finds pant → Tries to match → Goes to shoes → Overwhelmed →
ABANDONS CART
```

### Pain Points

**Decision Fatigue** — Users face 10,000+ individual SKUs with no guidance on what goes with what.
Browsing a catalog this large with no curation overwhelms users into indecision, and indecision converts to zero. The cognitive load of choosing one item is manageable; choosing four items that must visually work together compounds that load multiple times over.

**Poor Outfit Matching** — No guarantee pieces work together visually.
Without styling expertise or a tool that validates color and style compatibility, users are left guessing whether a shirt and pant they've separately selected will actually look right together — a guess many aren't confident enough to act on.

**Low Conversion** — Average fashion e-commerce CVR is 1–2%.
This is the direct commercial consequence of the friction above: only 1–2 out of every 100 visitors complete a purchase, among the lowest conversion rates of any e-commerce vertical.

**High Return Rate** — 30–40% of returns are due to mismatch.
A meaningful share of returns aren't about size or quality — they happen because the item didn't actually go with what the customer already owns or intended to pair it with, an error that's invisible until the package arrives.

**No Styling Expertise** — Regular users don't know how to style looks.
Most shoppers are not trained stylists. They know what they like in isolation but struggle to translate that into a coordinated outfit — a gap traditional catalogs do nothing to close.

**Time Consuming** — Average fashion session is 20–30 minutes with no purchase.
Users spend half an hour or more cross-referencing products across categories, comparing, and second-guessing — and frequently leave with nothing in the cart, having burned both their time and the platform's acquisition cost.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Average fashion e-commerce conversion rate | 1–2% |
| Returns caused by outfit mismatch | 30–40% |
| Avg. session length with no purchase | 20–30 min |

## 2.2 Zylook's Solution

Zylook collapses the entire multi-step assembly process into a single decision: do you like this look or not. Instead of shopping for parts, users shop for finished outfits — curated by AI and stylists — and can purchase the complete combo in one action.

**Zylook Flow:**

```
User lands → Browses complete outfit → Loves the look →
One-click purchase entire outfit → Done in < 3 minutes
```

### Solution Pillars

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| Curated Combos | Expert + AI-styled complete outfit bundles, pre-validated for color, fit, and occasion compatibility. |
| AI Stylist | Conversational assistant that gives personalized outfit picks based on natural-language requests. |
| Outfit Builder | Lets users swap individual pieces within a combo without breaking the overall look or styling logic. |
| Visual Search | Users upload a reference image and the platform finds matching or similar outfit combos in the catalog. |
| Creator Economy | Influencers and stylists build outfit combos on the platform and earn commission when they sell. |
| Subscription | Zylook Plus unlocks premium styling access, early drops, and exclusive AI stylist features. |

## 2.3 Outfit Combo Categories

The outfit catalog is organized into six taxonomy groups so users can discover combos by occasion, season, and identity rather than by individual product type. This structure also drives the filtering logic on the browse and search experience.

